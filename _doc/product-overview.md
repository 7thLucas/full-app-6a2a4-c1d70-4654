# Product Overview — MyCRM

> Single source of truth for the product. Some details below are early working
> assumptions (marked _assumption_) and will firm up as scoping is confirmed.

## What it is
A lightweight CRM for keeping every relationship moving forward. It does three
things well: store **contacts**, keep a running **log of interactions** with each
one, and show the **deal pipeline** at a glance. Deliberately simple — no bloat,
no enterprise configuration.

## Who it's for
A **single solo operator** running their own sales and relationships —
a freelancer, consultant, agency owner, or solo founder. Strictly single-user:
no team seats, no collaboration. Someone who currently tracks leads in their head,
a spreadsheet, or scattered notes and loses follow-ups as a result.

## The problem it solves
Deals slip silently. A warm lead goes cold because a follow-up was forgotten, and
there is no single place that shows what is stalling. Recovering even one deal a
month typically pays for the system many times over.

## Core capabilities (MVP)
**Day-one hero:** the **pipeline board** — the one thing the product must nail
first. Contacts and the interaction timeline exist to feed it.

1. **Contacts** — a clean directory of people and companies with the essentials.
2. **Interaction timeline** — log calls, emails, and meetings per contact so the
   full history of a relationship is one click away.
3. **Pipeline board** — deals as cards that move across stages (e.g. Lead →
   Contacted → Proposal → Won/Lost) so nothing stalls unseen.

## Strategic principles
- **Stay simple.** Every feature must earn its place against "is this still a
  simple CRM?" Resist enterprise creep.
- **Surface what's stalling.** The product's job is to make a forgotten
  follow-up impossible to miss.
- **Fast to log.** Capturing an interaction must take seconds, or it won't happen.

## Tone & brand _(assumption — to confirm)_
Calm, clean, confident. Productivity-tool aesthetic — uncluttered, fast, trustworthy.

## Out of scope (for now)
Marketing automation, email sending/sequencing, invoicing, and complex reporting.
These are deliberate non-goals for the MVP.
